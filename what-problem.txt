What problem are you solving, and why does it matter?

Every system that determines truth today — oracles, prediction markets, moderation, governance — assumes someone can eventually verify the answer. For the hardest and fastest-growing category of questions, that assumption breaks. Fake news, subjective disputes, AI disagreements, and long-horizon forecasts have no verifiable ground truth. No data feed to check. No authority to ask. If the outcome is unverifiable, existing infrastructure simply cannot resolve it.

This is not a niche gap. It is the default state of most important questions. And the consequence is that truth either gets decided by centralized power or doesn't get decided at all.

The root cause is architectural: truth infrastructure was built on verification. We remove that requirement entirely. The SKC mechanism — peer-reviewed Harvard research, ACM EC 2025 — uses game theory to make honest reporting the mathematically dominant strategy, even when the outcome can never be checked.

The internet has infrastructure for payments, identity, and data. It has no infrastructure for truth. We're building it.
