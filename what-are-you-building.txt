What are you building?

Today's truth infrastructure has a single point of failure: it needs a verifiable answer. But fake news, subjective disputes, AI disagreements, and long-term forecasts don't come with one. Unverifiable means unresolvable. Every existing system breaks.

We're building a protocol that resolves outcomes nobody can verify.

Yiling Protocol implements the SKC mechanism — a game-theoretic design from Harvard research published at ACM EC 2025. Agents sequentially report probabilities, each posting a bond. A random stopping rule means any agent could be last. The final report becomes the reference truth, and cross-entropy scoring rewards those who moved toward it.

The result: a strict Perfect Bayesian Equilibrium where honest reporting is the mathematically dominant strategy. No oracle. No arbiter. Just math.

This unlocks problems no existing system can touch: fake news verification, content authenticity, DAO governance, dispute resolution, AI data labeling, subjective oracles, and decentralized fact-checking — all resolved through game theory, not trusted third parties.

Prediction markets are one application. The protocol is the primitive.
