Who are your closest comparables and what do you understand that they don't?

The closest comparables fall into three categories: prediction markets (Polymarket/UMA, Augur), dispute resolution (Kleros, Reality.eth), and content verification (X Community Notes). All of them share one architectural assumption: a verifiable answer exists and someone — an oracle, a jury, or a moderator — can eventually check it.

Polymarket uses UMA's Optimistic Oracle, where token holders vote on outcomes. In March 2025, a single whale controlling ~25% of UMA voting power falsely resolved a $7M market. UMA's own documentation states it is "not suitable for data that is purely subjective." Kleros uses randomly selected jurors who vote based on Schelling point convergence — guess what the majority will guess. When no objective answer exists, the focal point disappears and the mechanism degrades to opinion polling. Community Notes uses bridging-based matrix factorization to surface cross-partisan consensus. It has no financial stakes, takes 24+ hours, and conflates "agreeable across groups" with "true." Musk himself acknowledged it is "increasingly being gamed."

Every one of these systems breaks at the same point: when truth cannot be externally verified.

We understand something they don't. You don't need to verify the outcome to incentivize honest reporting about it. The SKC mechanism — peer-reviewed at ACM EC 2025 — uses sequential reporting with random stopping so every agent could be last. The final agent has seen all prior information and is maximally informed, serving as a natural proxy for truth. Cross-entropy scoring then rewards each agent based on how much they moved the price toward that reference. The result is a strict Perfect Bayesian Equilibrium: honest reporting is not one strategy among many — it is the mathematically dominant one, even when the outcome can never be checked.

Existing systems ask "who do we trust to tell us the truth?" We ask "how do we make lying irrational?" That is a fundamentally different question, and it is the one that scales.
